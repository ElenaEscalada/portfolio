Description

Postcard illustration for "No more stolen sisters" campaign. According to statistics missing and murdered indigenous women and girls in Canada are bleak and overwhelming. Indigenous women usually have challenges and discrimination. It is said they are facing a “doble-burden” for being discriminated for being a woman, and further for being Indigenous. A 2015 United Nations report found that young aboriginal women are five times more likely to die under violent circumstances, as compared with non-Indigenous women. Moreover, the law is unfair and does not protect Indigenous women and girls acting differently with them than with non-Indigenous women and girls since their aggressors mostly go unpunished.

My goal is to educate people about the actual dilemma of missing and murdered indigenous women and girls in Canada. Also, I want to show that indigenous woman and girls are resilient, worthy and intelligent, who deserve to live, be protected and thrive like any woman or girl in the world.

To solve a problem, you have to be aware of it, so it is crucial to raise awareness in Canadian people of this situation to build a solution together as a nation.
